Enumerable#group_by

August 18th, 2015

The group_by enumerator function has tons of functionability in Ruby. The ruby docs describes it as an enumerator that takes a collection and some condition about that collection, and returns a hash that gives keys of each possible outcome, and a value containing an array with each item in the collection that resulted in the key. While this might seem like a complex explaination, it actually rather straight forward and powerful. Lets look at an example.

Lets say you have you wanted to look at the states of the United States and determine which ones had had over 4 million poeple living in them. You could do something like this: 


	     	([hash of states with population]).group_by {|state, population| population > 4000000}

This type of problem should give a hash with two keys, true and false, with the values being an array containing the hashes that were the states with over or under 4million poeple.
