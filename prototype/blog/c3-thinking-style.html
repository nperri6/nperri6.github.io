There are many different ways to classify the ways we act and think. One interesting way to look at how you think is with the Gregorc Thinking Style After taking this test myself, I learned that I am mostly an abstract sequential thinker.

Abstract Sequential Thinking

Abstract sequential thinking is mostly defined by someone who:

  • likes to be heard
  • analyzes situations before making decisions
  • applys logic in solving or finding solutions to problems
  • likes to have access to experts and stimulating environments, but is also able to work alone

AS thinkers generally struggle with:

  • Being forced to work with those of different views
  • having too little time to work through a subject
  • have to deal with lots of specific rules, repeating tasks, expressing their eomotions, or engaging with sentimental thinking

I definitely agree that a lot of these traits have summed up my experiences so far in DBC. I enjoy talking with other people and helping them solve their answers. I have had a fun time working through the ruby problems and addressing problems that poeple are having.

I do feel that I haven't had enough time to work through a lot of the HTML and CSS stuff so I would like to revisit that stuff in the future. Luckily, I have done a bit of Ruby in the past so I do feel comfortable with the amount of time that we have been given to learn it. I do find myself sometimes struggling to fill out the reflections, or even blog, as it isn't necessarily what I find most enjoyable about programming. (though I definitely see the value in it!)
